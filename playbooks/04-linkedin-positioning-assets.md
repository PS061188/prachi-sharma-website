# LinkedIn Positioning and Asset System

## Goal

Use LinkedIn as a clean extension of the website: broad AI positioning, credible writing-led authority, and selective consulting visibility.

## Positioning Rules

- Do not present the profile as healthcare-only.
- Emphasize:
  - AI evaluation
  - applied AI
  - LLM quality / workflow design
  - technical writing
- Keep wording broad enough for multiple domains, but specific enough to sound real.

## Featured Link Rules

- The website should appear in `Featured`.
- Use short, mobile-friendly title and description text.
- Avoid overly long descriptions that get truncated awkwardly.

## Suggested Website Card Style

- Short title
- Short description
- Clean social image
- No overloaded copy

## Consulting Visibility

- It is okay to say Prachi is open to consulting.
- Good consulting categories:
  - AI evaluation
  - LLM quality and reliability
  - prompt / workflow design
  - technical writing and explainers
  - guest talks / workshops where relevant

## Profile Photo Rules

- Best LinkedIn profile photos are close-up, face-forward, and calm.
- Full-body scenic portraits are usually weaker than clean close-up crops.
- Busy backgrounds reduce professionalism unless tightly cropped.
- Do not use generative “enhancement” that changes identity.
- Non-generative sharpening, crop cleanup, and subtle retouch are acceptable.

## Cover Image Rules

- In this field, simple is stronger than loud.
- A plain or visually restrained banner is often better than a text-heavy self-branding banner.
- Scenic banners can work if they crop well and do not fight with the profile image.

## Posting Rules

- Midweek is the preferred LinkedIn posting window.
- A single compromise slot for India + US audiences: Wednesday evening IST / US morning.
- Posts should have a strong first 2 to 3 lines and a clear bridge into the linked article.

## Writing for LinkedIn

- Strong hook first
- Then the concrete problem or failure mode
- Then the lesson
- Then the article title / link
- Avoid sounding like generic personal-brand copy

## Completion Checklist

- Featured card looks clean on mobile
- Website positioning matches LinkedIn positioning
- Consulting language is visible but not desperate
- Profile image feels professional
- Cover image feels restrained and credible
