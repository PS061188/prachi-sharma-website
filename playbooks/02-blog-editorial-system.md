# Blog Editorial System

## Goal

Write technical and analytical blog posts that feel polished, source-grounded, and editorial, while preserving the intellectual work from original notes and PDFs.

## Writing Tone

- Intelligent but readable
- Serious without sounding stiff
- Research-aware without sounding like raw documentation
- Warmly authoritative rather than generic or salesy

## What to Avoid

- Generic “definition + toy example + conclusion” writing
- Flattening careful notes into textbook prose
- Repeating the same claim in headline, dek, and first paragraph
- Explicitly saying “my notes” in public-facing deep dives
- Using screenshots of full PDF pages when actual figures can be extracted

## Structural Rules

- Anchor posts should give the bigger framework first.
- Supporting deep dives should go deeper into one metric or one concept.
- Use clear hierarchy:
  - main section headings
  - supporting tagline/subheading
  - sub-sections only where they help
- Do not over-segment with unnecessary headings.

## Technical Blog Content Rules

- Preserve the core order and substance of strong original documentation when it is valuable.
- Polish transitions aggressively so sections do not feel stitched together.
- Explain why the metric matters, not just what it is.
- Where appropriate, connect metric mechanics to product implications.
- Use examples that illuminate precision/recall/semantic tradeoffs clearly.

## Citations and References

- Keep one reference style across all blog posts.
- In-text paper mentions should be clickable and jump to the exact reference entry.
- Reference lists should feel intentional and consistent across posts.
- Choose papers that strengthen the post, not just papers that exist.

## Figures and Diagrams

- Prefer extracted figures, actual charts, or custom visuals over PDF screenshots.
- Every figure should sit in a logical narrative place.
- Captions should clarify what the figure is doing, not just restate its title.
- If a figure comes from a paper, credit it properly in the caption and references.

## Pull Quotes and Highlights

- Use one pull-quote treatment consistently across posts.
- Highlight only real high-signal lines.
- Do not overstyle every paragraph.

## TOC / Heading Rules

- The article TOC should list main section headings, not every small subheading.
- Heading hierarchy must be visually obvious.
- Supporting taglines under headings should wrap naturally and use paragraph-like width.

## Final Polish Standard

Before a post is considered done, check:

- Is the opening distinctive?
- Does it feel source-grounded rather than generic?
- Are transitions smooth?
- Are references consistent?
- Are figures placed correctly?
- Are there any abrupt jumps from concept to code or from theory to package details?
- Does the article feel like the work of someone who truly studied the subject?
