# Website Build System

## Goal

Build and maintain a personal website for Prachi Sharma that feels thoughtful, credible, editorial, and technically serious without becoming narrow, flashy, or generic.

## Positioning Rules

- Position Prachi broadly around `AI Evaluation`, `Applied AI`, `LLM quality`, `technical writing`, and `real-world AI systems`.
- Do not position her as working only or especially in healthcare.
- Healthcare work can stay as a real project/domain example, but not as the dominant brand identity.
- Prefer language that sounds research-informed and production-aware rather than startup-hype-heavy.

## Visual Direction

- Overall look should feel editorial, calm, and high-trust.
- Avoid cluttered layouts, purple-heavy AI aesthetics, or “AI slop” visuals.
- Use strong typography, generous whitespace, and restrained accents.
- Homepage, article pages, cards, and diagrams should feel like one system.

## Homepage Rules

- Hero should communicate breadth and seriousness in one glance.
- Projects should feel curated, not dumped.
- Services should be useful, specific, and broader than one domain.
- Writing section should support authority, not just activity.
- The site should read as the work of someone who thinks deeply and ships carefully.

## Services Rules

- Services should reflect consulting, technical writing, evaluation, prompt/workflow design, and talks/workshops where relevant.
- Avoid healthcare-specific service naming unless a service is explicitly healthcare-only.
- Keep service descriptions concrete and outcome-oriented.

## Project Rules

- Show strong project framing, not just titles.
- Each project should communicate problem, method, and why it matters.
- Keep project cards visually consistent.
- Avoid overclaiming.

## About / Career Narrative

- Keep the career story broad enough for cross-domain AI work.
- Highlight real work in healthcare, evaluation, and LLM systems without trapping the profile in one niche.
- Tone should be reflective, capable, and grounded.

## Blog Integration Rules

- Blog should feel native to the site, not bolted on.
- Articles should inherit the same seriousness and visual language as the homepage.
- Blog cards, article headers, metadata, and references should look consistent.

## Editing Principles

- Preserve real substance from source notes and PDFs.
- Avoid generic filler explanations when stronger source-grounded reasoning exists.
- Make writing web-readable without losing the sense that serious study happened.
- Prefer clarity over jargon, but do not flatten technical nuance.

## Quality Bar

The site should feel:

- credible
- calm
- technically literate
- visually intentional
- usable on mobile and desktop

It should not feel:

- overdesigned
- niche-locked
- buzzwordy
- copied from generic portfolio templates

## Completion Checklist

- Positioning is broad and coherent
- Services match the desired consulting identity
- Projects and writing support the same story
- Blog pages feel integrated with the site
- Visual language is consistent
- Metadata and sharing experience are handled
- Live site reflects the final decisions
