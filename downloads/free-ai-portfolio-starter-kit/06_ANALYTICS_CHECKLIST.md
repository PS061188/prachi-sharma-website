# Analytics Checklist for Your AI Portfolio

A portfolio should not only look good.

It should help you learn:
- who is visiting
- what they read
- what they ignore
- what makes them click
- what makes them contact you

## Website analytics

☐ Have you installed analytics on your website?

☐ Can you see total visitors?

☐ Can you see page-wise views?

☐ Can you see traffic sources?

☐ Can you see how many visitors come from LinkedIn?

☐ Can you see which blog posts get attention?

☐ Can you see whether people visit your Contact or Work With Me page?

## Blog analytics

☐ Which blog post gets the most views?

☐ Which blog has the longest reading time?

☐ Which blog gets clicks from LinkedIn?

☐ Which topic seems to create most interest?

☐ Which blog should be turned into more posts?

## LinkedIn analytics

☐ Which posts get profile visits?

☐ Which posts get website clicks?

☐ Which posts get saves?

☐ Which posts get comments from relevant people?

☐ Which posts bring DMs?

## Track LinkedIn-to-website clicks

When you share your website, blog, or project page on LinkedIn, check whether people actually click through.

Simple things to track:

☐ Did profile views increase after the post?

☐ Did website visits increase on the same day or next day?

☐ Did anyone visit your project, blog, or contact page?

☐ Did the post lead to comments, saves, DMs, or useful conversations?

You do not need advanced tracking at the beginning. Start by noticing whether LinkedIn activity leads people toward your website.

## Simple monthly review

At the end of each month, ask:

1. Which page got the most visits?
2. Which post brought the most profile views?
3. Which topic got the best response?
4. Which page needs better copy?
5. Which topic should I write more about?
