# LinkedIn Positioning Checklist for AI Professionals

Your LinkedIn profile should not repeat your resume.

It should quickly show:
- what you understand
- what kind of problems you work on
- what you write about
- why someone should trust your judgment

## Headline

☐ Does your headline say more than your job title?

☐ Does it include your core themes?

Examples:
- AI Evaluation
- Applied AI
- LLM Quality
- Real-world AI Systems
- Technical Writing

Weak:
Senior Data Scientist | AI Enthusiast

Stronger:
Applied AI & LLM Evaluation | Data Science | Technical Writing on Real-World AI Systems

## About section

☐ Does your About section sound clear and human?

☐ Does it explain your work without too much jargon?

☐ Does it connect your experience to your current direction?

☐ Does it avoid sounding desperate for opportunities?

## Featured section

☐ Have you added your website?

☐ Is the title short enough for mobile?

☐ Is the description clear?

☐ Does the preview image look clean?

## Featured card copy examples

Use short copy because LinkedIn truncates previews quickly, especially on mobile.

Example 1:

Title:
AI Systems, Evaluation, and Applied AI

Description:
Projects and writing on LLM evaluation, applied AI, and real-world AI systems.

Example 2:

Title:
AI Portfolio and Technical Writing

Description:
Case studies, essays, and notes on model evaluation, NLP, and practical AI systems.

Example 3:

Title:
Real-World AI Systems

Description:
Selected work across AI evaluation, language systems, and production reliability.

## Posting

☐ Does your post start with a strong first 2–3 lines?

☐ Does it talk about a concrete problem?

☐ Does it share a lesson or point of view?

☐ Does it connect to your website, blog, or project?

☐ Does it avoid generic personal-brand language?

## Analytics

☐ Are you checking profile views after posting?

☐ Are you checking website clicks from LinkedIn?

☐ Are you noticing which post topics get saves or comments?

☐ Are you tracking whether people visit your Featured link?

☐ Are you learning from comments and DMs, not just likes?
